# timescale separation

status: current (as of 2026-04-16).

the brain is not a single clock. it is a hierarchy of clocks -- delta (0.5-4 Hz), theta (4-8 Hz), alpha (8-13 Hz), beta (13-30 Hz), gamma (30-100 Hz) -- running simultaneously, coupled through phase-amplitude interactions, and dynamically modulated by neuromodulatory broadcast signals. this multi-timescale architecture separates information by temporal resolution: gamma carries fine-grained local computation in ~25 ms frames ([[gamma_oscillations]]), theta organizes sequential items into ~125 ms working memory buffers ([[theta_oscillations]]), alpha gates information flow through top-down suppression ([[neural_synchrony]]), and the neuromodulatory systems tune all of these on a timescale of seconds to minutes ([[neuromodulatory_framework]]). every synapse adds its own temporal filtering through short-term depression and facilitation ([[short_term_plasticity]]), and the sleep cycle imposes a consolidation timescale of hours ([[memory_consolidation]]).

todorov has at most two timescales, both fixed after training. this synthesis examines what that gap costs, what it saves, and whether any intervention is worth the complexity.

## the biological principle

### nested timescales and their computational functions

the computational power of biological timescale separation comes from three properties that build on each other.

**property 1: concurrent operation.** all frequency bands run simultaneously in the same neural tissue. a single pyramidal neuron in hippocampal CA1 participates in gamma cycles (~25 ms), theta cycles (~125 ms), and sharp-wave ripples (~50-100 ms at 100-250 Hz), contributing different information at each timescale. the gamma cycle provides a local competitive selection window -- the PING mechanism selects the most strongly driven neurons within each ~25 ms frame ([[gamma_oscillations]]). the theta cycle organizes ~5-8 of these gamma frames into a sequential buffer, implementing the lisman-jensen theta-gamma code with capacity ~ f_theta / f_gamma ~ 7 items ([[theta_oscillations]]). this is not sequential processing at different speeds. it is parallel processing at different resolutions, operating on the same substrate at the same time.

**property 2: cross-frequency coupling.** the frequency bands are not independent. theta phase modulates gamma amplitude through phase-amplitude coupling (PAC): gamma power peaks near the theta trough and is minimal near the theta peak ([[neural_synchrony]]). this nesting solves a fundamental scale problem. slow oscillations can synchronize across large distances (long-range theta coherence between hippocampus and prefrontal cortex) but are too coarse for fine-grained computation. fast oscillations support precise local computation but cannot synchronize over long distances due to conduction delays. PAC bridges these scales -- global coordination from theta, local computation from gamma. the cross-frequency coupling is not just a passive correlation. it is actively regulated: attention increases gamma power within specific theta phases, and neuromodulatory state shifts the coupling strength.

**property 3: dynamic modulation.** none of the frequencies are fixed. gamma frequency increases ~3 Hz with selective attention (fries 2001). theta frequency tracks running speed in navigating rodents. acetylcholine shifts the balance between encoding (theta trough, high plasticity) and retrieval (theta peak, low plasticity) phases within each theta cycle (hasselmo 2002). norepinephrine modulates neural gain, changing the effective bandwidth of processing -- phasic NE sharpens responses (exploitation), tonic NE broadens them (exploration) ([[neuromodulatory_framework]]). at every synapse, short-term depression and facilitation add activity-dependent temporal filtering with time constants from 50 ms to 800 ms ([[short_term_plasticity]]). the tsodyks-markram model captures these dynamics with just two state variables (available vesicles x, release probability u), but the diversity of tau_d and tau_f across synapse types creates a rich bank of temporal filters at every connection.

**property 4: communication gating by timescale.** different frequency bands serve different communication directions. gamma coherence gates feedforward communication (V1 to V4), originating from superficial cortical layers. alpha-beta coherence gates feedback communication (higher to lower areas), originating from deep layers ([[neural_synchrony]]). this means the timescale of an oscillation encodes its computational role: fast oscillations carry bottom-up sensory data, slow oscillations carry top-down predictions and context. the communication-through-coherence framework (fries 2005, 2015) formalizes this: two populations communicate effectively only when they are synchronized at appropriate frequencies. the same physical synapse transmits or blocks depending on the phase alignment of sender and receiver gamma oscillations, gated by the unequal duty cycle (~3-5 ms excitatory window vs ~10-20 ms inhibitory window in each gamma cycle).

**property 5: per-synapse temporal filtering.** beyond the network-level oscillatory timescales, each individual synapse adds its own temporal filtering through short-term plasticity. depressing synapses (tau_d >> tau_f, large baseline release probability U) act as high-pass filters, responding strongly to onsets and attenuating sustained activity -- equivalent to the kda alpha decay where old state is exponentially forgotten. facilitating synapses (tau_f >> tau_d, small U) act as low-pass integrators, responding weakly to isolated events but building up over repeated activation ([[short_term_plasticity]]). the diversity of STP profiles across synapse types (depressing at excitatory-to-excitatory, facilitating at excitatory-to-Martinotti cell) means that information arriving at a single neuron is simultaneously filtered through multiple temporal windows at the synaptic level, before any network-level oscillatory processing occurs. this is the finest-grained timescale separation in the brain -- one that todorov partially captures through per-channel alpha but entirely misses for the facilitation dimension.

the result is a system where timescale is not an architectural parameter set before operation. it is a dynamically controlled variable, adjusted online by neuromodulatory state, attention, and activity history.

## the todorov instantiation

todorov has three layer types, each with different temporal dynamics.

**kda (18/24 layers): exponential decay, no oscillation.** the delta-rule recurrence maintains a matrix-valued state S_t = diag(alpha) * S_{t-1} + beta_t * k_t * v_t^T, where alpha = sigmoid(alpha_log) is a per-channel per-head decay rate and beta_t = sigmoid(beta_proj(x_t)) is a data-dependent write gate. alpha_log is initialized at N(-2.0, 0.5), giving initial alpha ~ 0.12 -- meaning 88% of state content decays per timestep. during training, gradients push alpha_log toward values that minimize cross-entropy loss. the resulting alpha distribution defines the architecture's primary timescale: some channels remember for ~5 tokens (alpha ~ 0.1), others for ~50 tokens (alpha ~ 0.9). but after training, these decay rates are fixed. a channel with alpha = 0.85 always decays at that rate, whether the model is processing a simple repeated pattern or a novel syntactic construction. this is analogous to short-term depression ([[short_term_plasticity]]) -- the kda alpha is functionally equivalent to the vesicle recovery timescale tau_d in the tsodyks-markram model -- but without the complementary facilitation mechanism (the u variable that increases with repeated activation). kda has depression without facilitation: one half of the temporal filtering story.

**mamba3 (3/24 layers): complex rotation at learned frequencies.** the state evolves as h_t = A_bar_t * h_{t-1} + B_bar_t * B_t * x_t, followed by rotation at angle = rope_freq * t. the d_state//2 = 16 dimension pairs each oscillate at an independently learned frequency. the dt_proj provides data-dependent modulation of the discretization timestep, affecting the decay rate but not the rotation frequency ([[oscillations_to_mamba3_rotation]]). rope_freq is a static nn.Parameter. the strongest interpretation of this rotation is not oscillatory dynamics in the biological sense but rather positional encoding for the recurrent state -- the SSM counterpart of RoPE in attention. the frequencies could in principle learn task-relevant timescales, but there is no cross-dimensional coupling (no PAC between "slow" and "fast" state dimensions), no inter-layer coherence (layers 4, 12, and 20 rotate independently), and no frequency modulation by input content.

**mla (3/24 layers): stateless exact retrieval.** softmax attention over compressed per-token representations. O(T^2) computation, no recurrent state, no temporal dynamics. mla provides the architecture's longest-range mechanism (full context window) but has no concept of timescale at all -- every token in the window is equally accessible. from a timescale perspective, mla is the limiting case: infinite memory (within the context window), zero decay, no temporal filtering. it is the opposite of kda's exponential forgetting.

the three systems create a crude timescale hierarchy: kda provides short-to-medium range temporal memory (tokens decay exponentially, effective range depends on alpha), mamba3 provides oscillatory dynamics at learned frequencies (if it survives the fused-kernel test), and mla provides exact retrieval with no temporal structure. but this hierarchy is accidental -- it emerges from the different mathematical formulations, not from a design principle of timescale separation. there is no mechanism that assigns different information to different timescales, no theta-gamma-like nesting that organizes kda state into temporal slots, and no dynamic routing that directs information toward the appropriate timescale based on content.

### the timescale inventory

biology operates at a minimum of 8 distinguishable timescales simultaneously:

- gamma (30-100 Hz, ~10-33 ms): local computation, competitive selection, binding
- beta (13-30 Hz, ~33-77 ms): feedback communication, motor planning
- alpha (8-13 Hz, ~77-125 ms): inhibitory gating, top-down suppression
- theta (4-8 Hz, ~125-250 ms): working memory organization, encoding/retrieval alternation
- delta (0.5-4 Hz, ~250-2000 ms): slow oscillation, cross-regional coordination
- short-term plasticity (50-800 ms): per-synapse temporal filtering via vesicle dynamics
- neuromodulatory control (seconds to minutes): online meta-parameter tuning
- consolidation (hours to months): hippocampal-cortical memory transfer during sleep

all dynamically modulated. all coupled through cross-frequency interactions.

todorov: kda alpha decay (per-channel, fixed after training), mamba3 rotation frequency (per-dimension, fixed after training). no coupling between them. no dynamic modulation of either.

## the gap

three capabilities are absent from todorov, each corresponding to a property of biological timescale separation.

**gap 1: no dynamic modulation.** kda alpha and mamba3 rope_freq are fixed parameters after training. they cannot adapt to the current input. the bridge analysis in [[neuromodulation_to_learning_and_gating]] details this precisely: alpha controls memory persistence (how long associations last in S_t), beta is data-dependent but sees only the current token x_t with no access to global state signals (running loss, attention entropy, spike density). the neuromodulatory framework ([[neuromodulatory_framework]]) suggests that biology has an entire online hyperparameter tuning system -- four broadcast signals (DA, ACh, NE, 5-HT) controlling meta-parameters in real time. todorov fixes all meta-parameters before training begins or follows a predetermined schedule (cosine lr decay). the adam optimizer provides per-parameter adaptive learning rates during training, which is more granular than biological neuromodulation -- but adam operates during backpropagation, not during inference. at inference time, todorov has no adaptive control whatsoever.

the practical consequence: a kda channel with alpha = 0.85 decays at the same rate whether processing a dialogue turn boundary (where rapid state update would help) or continuing a mid-sentence clause (where state preservation matters). biology would modulate: acetylcholine would rise at the turn boundary (encoding mode, fast forgetting, strong writes) and fall during continuation (retrieval mode, slow forgetting, read from state). this is the hasselmo encoding/retrieval alternation ([[theta_oscillations]]), operating within each theta cycle at ~125 ms resolution. todorov's beta_t provides a data-dependent write gate, which is the closest analog to this alternation, but beta sees only x_t -- it has no access to "is this a novelty boundary?" as a global state signal.

**gap 2: no cross-frequency coupling.** kda and mamba3 do not interact oscillatorily. kda has no intrinsic timescale -- its dynamics are driven entirely by the input sequence. mamba3's 16 dimension pairs rotate independently with no coupling. there is no mechanism analogous to theta-gamma PAC, where the phase of a slow process modulates the amplitude of a fast process. the biological significance of cross-frequency coupling is that it separates information by temporal resolution within a shared substrate ([[neural_synchrony]]). todorov represents all information at all timescales in a single undifferentiated residual stream. this may be adequate -- the residual stream is a more bandwidth-efficient communication channel than oscillatory coherence -- but it means todorov cannot implement the multiplexing that biology uses to manage multiple concurrent cognitive processes.

the oscillation-to-mamba3 bridge ([[oscillations_to_mamba3_rotation]]) considered adding PAC between mamba3 state dimensions (slow dimensions modulating fast dimensions' amplitude) but recommended against it: the biological motivation for PAC is multi-item working memory and spatial navigation, which maps poorly to language modeling where sentences far exceed the ~7-item theta-gamma capacity. the implementation would introduce multiple hyperparameters (which dimensions are slow vs fast, coupling strength, coupling function) with no principled way to set them. this is the right call for phase 5, but it leaves the gap open.

**gap 3: no consolidation.** kda state decays exponentially. this is forgetting, not consolidation. there is no process that transfers fast-learned associations from kda state (capacity-limited, continuously decaying) to a more permanent store ([[memory_consolidation]]). mla's compressed cache stores exact per-token representations, but there is no hippocampal-to-cortical transfer: no replay, no sleep-like offline processing, no schema-dependent learning rate adjustment. the two memory systems (kda state and mla cache) operate independently within the same forward pass with no information flow between them. in biological terms, todorov has a hippocampus (kda: fast, capacity-limited, forgetting) and a neocortex (mla: slow to write, permanent, exact) but no sleep. the sharp-wave ripple mechanism -- where hippocampal replay during slow-wave sleep trains cortical circuits to represent memories independently -- has no analog. the three-stage nesting (slow oscillation > spindle > ripple) that coordinates this transfer is itself a timescale separation mechanism, one that operates on the scale of hours rather than milliseconds.

## the mamba3 question

run_010 (kda+mla only, mamba3 dropped): bpb ratio 0.663x (33.7% better than transformer).
run_011 (full architecture, kda+mamba3+mla): bpb ratio 0.722x (27.8% better than transformer).

mamba3 hurts. adding the continuous-dynamics layer to the architecture worsened performance by ~6 percentage points. the sequential scan bottleneck (~15s/step at T=2048 with 267m params) makes it computationally prohibitive at scale.

the oscillation-to-mamba3 bridge ([[oscillations_to_mamba3_rotation]]) evaluated five possible modifications to make mamba3 more oscillation-like (data-dependent rope_freq, cross-frequency coupling in state, shared frequencies across layers, oscillatory spike thresholds, inter-layer coherence gating). the verdict on all five: do not implement. the biological motivation is weak for language modeling, the implementation risks are high, and the expected benefits are low.

the honest question is whether mamba3 provides any computational capability that kda+mla cannot replicate. the comparison article ([[oscillations_vs_recurrence]]) identifies the dissenting argument: oscillations solve coordination problems in parallel processing architectures, but todorov processes serially through a layer stack. the interference management that oscillations provide may be a solution to a problem todorov does not have.

from the timescale perspective, the mamba3 question reduces to: does an architecture need an explicit oscillatory timescale (periodic dynamics), or is the exponential decay timescale (aperiodic dynamics) sufficient? the biological evidence suggests both are important -- oscillatory and aperiodic dynamics serve different computational functions (binding vs. memory persistence, communication gating vs. temporal filtering). but the biological evidence comes from parallel architectures. in a serial layer stack, the binding-by-synchrony function is replaced by co-storage in the kda state matrix, and communication gating is replaced by the always-on residual stream ([[oscillations_vs_recurrence]]). the computational functions that oscillation serves in biology may already be covered by other mechanisms in todorov.

the supervisor decision: one more fused-kernel run with mamba_ssm. if the fused kernel eliminates the throughput bottleneck and performance improves (or at least does not degrade), mamba3 stays. if it still underperforms kda+mla, mamba3 is dropped from the architecture. this is the correct experimental design -- isolating the kernel implementation question from the architectural question. the current evidence is confounded: mamba3 might hurt because its sequential scan wastes training steps, not because continuous-dynamics layers are computationally inferior.

if mamba3 is dropped, todorov loses its only source of periodic dynamics. the architecture becomes purely aperiodic: exponential decay (kda alpha) plus stateless retrieval (mla). this is not necessarily a loss. the LinOSS architecture (oscillatory state-space models, 2024) explicitly parameterizes SSM dynamics as forced harmonic oscillators, and comparing its performance against kda's delta rule would be a more informative test of whether oscillatory dynamics add value -- but that comparison belongs in phase 6+, not the current sequence.

## the neuromodulator proposal

the bridge article ([[neuromodulation_to_learning_and_gating]]) proposes a concrete intervention: a neuromodulator network with 130 parameters per layer that reads global state signals and modulates kda alpha and beta.

architecture: Linear(4, 16) -> SiLU -> Linear(16, 2). inputs: running_loss_ema, attention_entropy, spike_density, state_norm. outputs: alpha_modulation and beta_modulation, each in [-1, 1], added to alpha_log and beta_proj output respectively before sigmoid.

this addresses gap 1 (no dynamic modulation) directly. the biological mapping is functional, not mechanistic: running_loss_ema ~ dopaminergic RPE, attention_entropy ~ cholinergic uncertainty, spike_density ~ noradrenergic arousal, state_norm ~ memory load. the expected behavior -- high loss triggers faster forgetting (discard misleading state) and stronger writes (encode surprising input) -- mirrors the cholinergic encoding/retrieval switch (hasselmo 2006).

parameter count: 130 per layer, 3120 total at 24 layers. negligible relative to 300m model parameters. computational cost: one Linear + SiLU + Linear per layer per token. zero-initialization ensures the default behavior is unchanged; learned modulation must improve loss to persist.

estimated probability of meaningful improvement: 15-25%. the primary risk is that the 4 input features may not carry enough information -- the residual stream already encodes rich contextual information that beta_proj accesses. the confound risk: any improvement could be attributed to 130 additional parameters rather than the neuromodulatory structure.

the most informative experiment design, as specified in [[neuromodulation_to_learning_and_gating]]: a 4-way comparison. (a) baseline: fixed alpha, input-gated beta. (b) neuromod_net modulating alpha only. (c) neuromod_net modulating beta only. (d) neuromod_net modulating both. plus a capacity control: 130 params added to a wider projection instead of the neuromod_net. this isolates whether the benefit (if any) comes from dynamic alpha, dynamic beta, their interaction, or simply more parameters.

phase: 6+ (after phase 5 sequence completes). this is not a priority -- it is a research direction informed by the timescale gap.

## within-layer recurrence

a mechanism not yet explored in todorov that could address timescale separation without adding new parameters: repeated application of the kda read-update cycle within a single layer.

the idea: instead of applying the kda delta rule once per token per layer, apply it k times. each iteration reads the current state (y = S * q), updates the state based on the read (S = S + beta * k * v^T with v derived from y), and reads again. this creates attractor dynamics -- the state converges toward representations that are self-consistent under repeated read-update.

biological analog: recurrent cortical circuits process information through multiple passes of excitation and inhibition within a single cortical column before propagating to the next area. the gamma cycle itself involves multiple sub-cycles of competitive selection. theta phase precession compresses multi-step trajectories into a single theta cycle ([[theta_oscillations]]). more directly, the hippocampal sharp-wave ripple replays a stored sequence in ~50-100 ms through iterative reactivation of the CA3 recurrent network ([[memory_consolidation]]) -- this is iterated read-update at its most literal.

the timescale connection: k=2 within-layer recurrence effectively doubles the effective recurrence depth without adding layers, creating a separation between "fast" single-pass processing (k=1, the current behavior) and "slow" iterative convergence (k>1). this is not cross-frequency coupling in the biological sense, but it introduces a second processing timescale within the same layer. the biological analog is more precise than it appears: the gamma PING cycle itself is an iterated read-update. pyramidal cells fire (read), interneurons provide feedback inhibition (update), and the survivors fire again in the next cycle (read again). the iteration implements competitive selection through temporal unfolding -- exactly what k>1 recurrence would do in the kda state.

the user decision: worth a quick k=2 test at 6m scale. the evidence bar is high: mathematical identity (must reduce to current behavior at k=1) + ablation necessity (k=2 must outperform k=1 at matched compute) + quantitative prediction (specify expected bpb improvement before running). cost: one additional matmul per kda layer per token. risk: the extra recurrence step might not provide useful error correction if the single-pass delta rule already converges. the key confound to control for: k=2 at 18 kda layers means 36 effective recurrence steps. is this equivalent to 36 kda layers at k=1 with half the width? the test must match total compute, not just parameter count.

## challenges and counter-arguments

**challenge 1: the serial architecture may not need timescale separation.** the strongest argument against importing biological timescale mechanisms is architectural. biological oscillations solve coordination and interference problems that arise from massively parallel processing ([[oscillations_vs_recurrence]]). todorov processes through a serial layer stack where no two layers are active simultaneously. the residual stream provides full-bandwidth, deterministic communication between all layers -- more powerful than the noisy, phase-jitter-limited channel that oscillatory coherence provides. the absence of oscillatory timescale structure may be a feature of serial architectures, not a deficit. the evidence that kda+mla alone achieves 0.663x bpb ratio (run_010) without any oscillatory mechanism supports this: strong performance does not require multiple concurrent timescales.

**challenge 2: fixed timescales may be sufficient for fixed distributions.** biological dynamic modulation evolved for non-stationary environments -- an animal must adapt its learning rate when the environment changes, modulate its temporal horizon when reward contingencies shift, adjust exploration when established strategies fail. a language model trained on a fixed dataset has no such need. the cosine lr schedule and gradient-optimized alpha may be adequate compromises: they find values that work on average across the training distribution. whether online modulation provides benefits within a single training run -- rather than across a lifetime of non-stationary experience -- is an open empirical question. the neuromodulator proposal's 15-25% probability of meaningful improvement reflects this uncertainty. the strongest version of this challenge: the adam optimizer already implements per-parameter adaptive learning rates (~300m independent rates at 300m params), which is more granular than any biological neuromodulatory system. adam is the neuromodulator, operating at training time, with far greater specificity than acetylcholine or norepinephrine could achieve ([[neuromodulation_to_learning_and_gating]]). the gap is only at inference time, where adam has no effect.

**challenge 3: the neuromodulator network may learn nothing.** zero-initialization means the neuromod_net starts by doing nothing. it will only learn modulation that improves cross-entropy loss. there is no guarantee that the biologically motivated behavior (high loss -> faster forgetting, high uncertainty -> stronger writes) is what emerges. the network might learn a trivial constant offset, or a pattern that is computationally useful but biologically uninterpretable. worse, it might not converge at all -- the multiplicative interaction between neuromod_net outputs and kda state dynamics could create training instabilities. the 130-parameter budget might be too small to learn meaningful modulation policies from 4 scalar inputs.

**challenge 4: todorov's per-channel alpha is already more granular than biological neuromodulation.** at 267m scale, kda has 1024 independent decay rates (16 heads * 64 channels). biological neuromodulation is a broadcast signal -- a single cholinergic projection modulates entire cortical regions. the proposed neuromod_net adds a single scalar modulation to alpha_log, which is LESS granular than the existing per-channel alpha. the proposal moves TOWARD biological coarseness, not away from it. this is defensible on biological grounds but computationally dubious: why would reducing the specificity of decay rate control improve performance? the strongest case is that the global modulation provides context that the per-channel alpha cannot access (because alpha is fixed, not input-dependent), but this context might be more effectively provided by enriching beta_proj's input features rather than adding a separate network. a simpler intervention: make beta_proj a function of [x_t; running_loss_ema; spike_density] instead of just x_t, giving the existing write gate access to global state without adding a new architectural component.

## the honest assessment

todorov operates at fewer concurrent timescales than biology, with no dynamic modulation and no cross-frequency coupling. this is a genuine architectural limitation, not a superficial naming gap.

the limitation may not matter for language modeling at current scale. the evidence is ambiguous: kda+mla alone (two fixed timescales, no oscillation) achieves 0.663x bpb ratio, which is the architecture's best result. adding mamba3 (the only layer with oscillatory dynamics) made it worse. the architecture's strength appears to come from the kda delta-rule associative memory and mla exact retrieval, not from temporal multiplexing.

the timescale hierarchy in todorov is: kda alpha (per-channel exponential decay, ~5 to ~50 tokens effective range), mamba3 rope_freq (per-dimension rotation, interpretation unclear, possibly positional rather than oscillatory), and mla (full context, no decay). two of these three are aperiodic. the third is periodic but may serve positional encoding rather than oscillatory dynamics. no mechanism couples them. no mechanism modulates them online.

the most defensible next steps are:

1. resolve the mamba3 question empirically (one more fused-kernel run). this determines whether continuous dynamics have any place in the architecture at all.
2. test within-layer recurrence at k=2, 6m scale. cheap, isolable, addresses the "second processing timescale" question without new parameters.
3. defer the neuromodulator proposal to phase 6+. the 15-25% probability of improvement does not justify priority over phase 5 completion.

the deeper question -- whether a fixed-timescale architecture hits a ceiling that a dynamically-modulated one would not -- cannot be answered at 300m scale. it requires tasks that demand adaptive temporal integration: multi-turn dialogue, streaming input, or non-stationary distributions. language modeling on a fixed corpus may be the wrong benchmark for timescale separation. the biological timescale hierarchy evolved under selection pressure from a non-stationary, multi-modal, real-time environment. an architecture trained on static text may simply not need what timescale separation provides.

but if todorov is ever extended to streaming, interactive, or multi-modal settings -- where context length is unbounded, input statistics shift over time, and multiple information streams compete for limited state capacity -- the timescale gap will matter. the neuromodulator proposal (130 params/layer, phase 6+) is the smallest intervention that addresses this gap. within-layer recurrence (k=2, 6m scale) is the cheapest test of whether a second processing timescale helps at all. neither should delay phase 5 completion, but both should be ready when the architectural limits of fixed timescales become empirically visible.
