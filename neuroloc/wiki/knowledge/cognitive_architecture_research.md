# cognitive architecture research

status: current (as of 2026-04-23).

curated research shelf on the structure of mind with an architecture-first bias. this page keeps only the parts of the literature that cash out into routing, memory access, broadcast, simulation, precision control, or staged development. broad philosophical claims stay out unless they are operationally useful.

## strongest takeaways

- working memory is best read as controlled access plus a small focus, not as one buffer
- attention is a routing and precision problem
- long-term memory, replay, and imagination belong in one family of state reconstruction and simulation operations
- workspace-style broadcast remains useful as a limited-bandwidth coordination story
- predictive processing and active inference are strongest when tied to concrete latent models and control loops
- self-model ideas are most useful when treated as control and monitoring structures rather than as narrative-only accounts
