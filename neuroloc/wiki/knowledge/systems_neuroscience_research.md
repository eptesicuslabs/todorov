# systems neuroscience research

status: current (as of 2026-04-23).

curated research shelf for systems and computational neuroscience with an architecture-first bias. this page collects literature and working notes on replay, hippocampal indexing, cortical recurrence, oscillatory routing, neuromodulatory control, and representational state formation. conclusions live in the synthesis pages; this page is the source shelf.

## strongest takeaways

- replay now looks more like a constructive consolidation and composition mechanism than a passive replay buffer alone
- hippocampus and entorhinal cortex encode temporal and contextual structure in addition to spatial state
- routing and access control appear repeatedly through thalamic loops, coherence, and modulatory state
- dendritic and compartmental computation remain relevant at system scale because they change what counts as the effective unit of computation

## load-bearing source clusters

### replay, indexing, and consolidation

- mcclelland, mcnaughton, and o'reilly 1995 — complementary fast and slow systems
- wilson and mcnaughton 1994 — replay during sleep
- ji and wilson 2007 — cortex-hippocampus coordinated replay
- pfeiffer and foster 2013 — awake goal-path replay
- bakermans et al. 2025 — replay as compositional future behavior

### hippocampal and entorhinal state formation

- o'keefe and dostrovsky 1971 — place coding baseline
- bakker et al. 2008 — dentate gyrus and ca3 separation evidence
- human hippocampal and entorhinal temporal-structure papers from 2024 — temporal organization and sequence structure
- 2025 hippocampus-to-entorhinal feedback work — direct novelty and routing loop

### cortical recurrence and routing

- rao and ballard 1999 — predictive coding baseline
- chadwick et al. 2022 — recurrent dynamics align to task-relevant structure
- shin et al. 2025 — recurrent pattern completion in sensory inference
- stout et al. 2024 — synchronized rhythms bias memory-guided decisions
- causal functional maps of brain rhythms in working memory 2024 — frequency-specific control hubs

### neuromodulation as control

- doya 2002 — neuromodulation as metalearning control
- dopamine-dependent ltp and effort-related cholinergic control papers from 2024-2025
- modern novelty and effort papers linking acetylcholine, dopamine, and state control
