# cross scale building blocks research

status: current (as of 2026-04-23).

curated shelf for reusable computational primitives that recur across scales. this page is not a catalog of subfields. it is a catalog of repeated moves: compete, gate, recur, route, index, replay, compress, and stabilize.

## candidate primitive set

- competition and normalization
- conditional gating and disinhibition
- recurrent reverberation and attractor maintenance
- routing by synchrony or effective coupling
- indexing plus separation and completion
- sparse coding plus compression and expansion
- replay and offline reinstatement
- homeostasis and set-point regulation
- global control signals and gain scheduling
- complementary fast and slow systems

## load-bearing sources

- carandini and heeger 2012 on divisive normalization
- olshausen and field 1996 plus later sparse-coding lines
- saalmann et al. 2012 and schmitt et al. 2017 on gating and coordination
- wang 2001 and later reverberation work
- marr 1971 plus teyler and discenna 1986 on indexing
- wilson and mcnaughton 1994 onward on replay
- turrigiano work on homeostasis
- aston-jones and cohen 2005 on adaptive gain

## why this shelf matters

it provides a better search space for neuroloc than one-off named techniques. the strongest cross-source conclusion so far is that the brain reuses a small bundle of primitives under different geometry, timescale, and control constraints.
