# Wulfram Gerstner

status: definitional. last fact-checked 2026-04-16.

Wulfram Gerstner is a computational neuroscientist at EPFL (Ecole Polytechnique Federale de Lausanne), best known for co-developing the [[adaptive_exponential]] (AdEx) model with Romain Brette in 2005 and for the open-access textbook "Neuronal Dynamics" (neuronaldynamics.epfl.ch).

key contributions:
- adaptive exponential integrate-and-fire model (Brette and Gerstner, 2005)
- spike response model (SRM) -- a general framework unifying integrate-and-fire variants
- "Neuronal Dynamics: From Single Neurons to Networks and Models of Cognition" (2014, Cambridge University Press) -- the primary reference textbook for this wiki
- systematic benchmarking of neuron model accuracy (Jolivet et al. 2008)

relevance to todorov: Gerstner's work provides the theoretical framework for understanding what ATMN is and what it is missing. the AdEx model's firing pattern classification (Naud et al. 2008, from Gerstner's group) maps the parameter space of 2-variable neuron models to behavioral outcomes -- exactly the kind of analysis needed to determine which features ATMN should incorporate. the "Neuronal Dynamics" textbook is the primary reference for the neuroloc wiki.
