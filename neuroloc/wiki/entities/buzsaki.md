# Gyorgy Buzsaki

status: definitional. last fact-checked 2026-04-16.

(b. 1949, Kaposvar, Hungary)

neuroscience research institute, NYU Langone Health. previously Rutgers University.

## contributions

the leading figure in neural oscillation research. his work established that brain oscillations are not epiphenomenal but are fundamental computational mechanisms.

key contributions:

- **neuronal oscillations in cortical networks** (Buzsaki and Draguhn, 2004, Science): foundational review establishing that cortical oscillations span five orders of magnitude in frequency, are phylogenetically preserved, and serve temporal representation, synaptic plasticity, and memory consolidation
- **Rhythms of the Brain** (Buzsaki, 2006, Oxford University Press): the definitive textbook on neural oscillations. covers the full frequency spectrum from slow oscillations (<1 Hz) through gamma (>30 Hz), with emphasis on the functional significance of cross-frequency coupling and the relationship between oscillations and cognitive function
- **mechanisms of gamma oscillations** (Buzsaki and Wang, 2012, Annual Review of Neuroscience): comprehensive review of PING and ING gamma generation, the role of PV+ interneurons, and the relationship between gamma and cognition
- **hippocampal sharp-wave ripples:** characterized the sharp-wave ripple complex (100-250 Hz oscillations coupled to sharp waves) during quiet wakefulness and slow-wave sleep. demonstrated that ripples replay behavioral sequences in compressed time, supporting memory consolidation
- **theta oscillations and place cells:** extensive work on hippocampal theta rhythm, theta phase precession, and the relationship between oscillations and spatial coding. contributed to understanding how theta sequences compress behavioral timescales for synaptic plasticity
- **log-scaling of brain oscillations:** proposed that brain oscillation frequencies are organized in a geometric (logarithmic) progression, with each frequency band approximately double the next lower band, and that this log-spacing reflects the need for non-interfering communication channels

## recognition

member of the National Academy of Sciences (USA), member of the Hungarian Academy of Sciences. the Brain Prize (2011), shared with Tamas Freund and Peter Somogyi for their work on hippocampal oscillations and interneurons. h-index >150.
