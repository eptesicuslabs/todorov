# Bernard Baars

status: definitional. last fact-checked 2026-04-16.

Bernard J. Baars. born 1946 in Amsterdam, Netherlands. cognitive scientist and neuroscientist. senior fellow at the Neurosciences Institute, San Diego (1998-2015). former affiliate fellow at the Center for Research on Consciousness and Cognition.

## education and career

studied psychology at UCLA (BA 1966, MA 1968, PhD 1970 under Gordon Bower, though Baars later diverged from behaviorism). postdoctoral work at Stony Brook University. faculty at the Wright Institute, Berkeley. research scientist at the Smith-Kettlewell Eye Research Institute, San Francisco. joined the Neurosciences Institute (founded by Gerald Edelman) in 1998.

## major contributions

### global workspace theory (1988)

Baars' central contribution is [[global_workspace_theory]] (GWT), first presented in "A Cognitive Theory of Consciousness" (Cambridge University Press, 1988). the theory proposes that the brain contains many specialized unconscious processors that operate in parallel, and that consciousness arises when information from one processor is selected and broadcast to a global workspace accessible by all other processors. the workspace is a functional hub, not a brain region: it enables inter-processor communication and flexible integration.

GWT was radical in 1988 for treating consciousness as a scientifically tractable cognitive function rather than a philosophical mystery. by defining consciousness as global availability of information (access consciousness), Baars sidestepped the hard problem and provided a framework that generates testable predictions about which stimuli will be consciously perceived.

### the theater metaphor

Baars popularized the theater metaphor for consciousness in "In the Theater of Consciousness" (Oxford University Press, 1997). conscious content is the bright spot on stage illuminated by the spotlight of attention. the audience (unconscious processors) receives the broadcast. backstage personnel (executive functions) shape what appears on stage. Baars explicitly rejected the Cartesian theater interpretation: there is no homunculus watching the stage.

### influence on the global neuronal workspace

Dehaene, Changeux & Naccache (2001, 2011) translated GWT into a specific neural architecture: the Global Neuronal Workspace (GNW) hypothesis. this identified the neural substrate (long-range cortico-cortical connections, prefrontal-parietal network), the mechanism (ignition via recurrent amplification), and the neural signatures (P3b ERP, late gamma, prefrontal sustained activity). the GNW became one of the two dominant scientific theories of consciousness (alongside [[integrated_information_theory|IIT]]).
