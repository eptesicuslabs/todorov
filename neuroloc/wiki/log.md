delta rule finding: KDA's name includes "delta" but the implementation does NOT perform erasure (the hallmark of delta rule learning). the update S_t = diag(alpha)*S_{t-1} + beta*k*v^T is exponential decay + additive write, not error-correcting write. true delta rule: S_t = S_{t-1} + beta*k*(v - S_{t-1}^T*k)^T*k, where the (v - S_{t-1}^T*k) term erases the old association before writing the new one. the name "kernel delta attention" is aspirational, not descriptive. updated bridge/plasticity_to_kda_delta_rule.md to reflect this

new bridge articles:
- bridge/positional_encoding_to_rope.md: four biological positional coding mechanisms (theta phase precession, grid cell phase offsets, tonotopic gradients, retinotopic maps) mapped to RoPE. phase precession is the strongest analog (both encode position as rotation angle in a periodic coordinate system). grid cell modular organization resembles multi-frequency RoPE (discrete spacing ratios ~1.4x vs geometric frequency bands). verdict: MODERATE analogy for phase precession, WEAK for the rest. RoPE was derived from mathematical principles (Su et al. 2021) not biology, but convergent solutions to the same problem (encoding sequential position as relative phase) are expected
- bridge/normalization_to_rmsnorm.md: four biological normalization mechanisms (divisive normalization, synaptic scaling, gain modulation, homeostatic plasticity) mapped to RMSNorm. RMSNorm is a degenerate special case of divisive normalization: uniform pool weights, exponent n=2, no semi-saturation constant, no temporal adaptation. it captures the scale-invariance property but not the computational richness (structured pools, surround suppression, contrast gain control). synaptic scaling (turrigiano 1998) is a closer functional analog: multiplicative rescaling that preserves relative magnitudes. verdict: RMSNorm implements the simplest possible normalization from a family of biologically rich operations

## 2026-04-06 spatial computation and navigation domain | mechanisms, comparison, bridge, entities, simulation

mechanism articles:
- mechanisms/place_cells.md: O'Keefe & Dostrovsky 1971. hippocampal CA1/CA3 pyramidal neurons that fire at specific locations. place fields ~30 cm diameter in rat, expand with environment size. discovery: cells encode location per se, not sensory input. O'Keefe & Nadel 1978 "The Hippocampus as a Cognitive Map" -- allocentric spatial representation. remapping: global (entire population reorganizes in different environment, driven by DG pattern separation), rate (location stable but firing rate changes with non-spatial context, Leutgeb et al. 2005), partial (intermediate). phase precession: O'Keefe & Recce 1993, spikes shift from late to early theta phase as animal traverses field, dual rate+phase coding, ~8-10x temporal compression within theta cycle enables STDP-window learning. inputs: MEC (grid cells, HD cells, border cells, speed cells) and LEC (non-spatial). Fourier synthesis model: place fields emerge as constructive interference of grid cells at multiple scales (Solstad et al. 2006). dorso-ventral gradient: small precise fields dorsally, large diffuse fields ventrally
- mechanisms/grid_cells.md: Hafting et al. 2005, Nature. MEC neurons firing in hexagonal (equilateral triangular) lattice. three properties: spacing (30-300 cm, dorso-ventral gradient), orientation (~3 discrete values, ~7.5 degrees apart), phase (unique per cell within module). modular organization: discrete modules sharing spacing/orientation but differing in phase, geometric spacing ratio ~1.4-1.7x between modules (Stensola et al. 2012), ~4-5 modules in rat. information capacity: exponential with number of modules. proposed function: path integration (dead reckoning). two model classes: continuous attractor networks (CAN -- hexagonal bump pattern from surround inhibition, velocity shifts bumps, toroidal topology confirmed by Gardner et al. 2022) and oscillatory interference (OI -- Burgess, Barry & O'Keefe 2007, constructive interference of 3 VCOs at 120-degree separations, frequency encodes velocity, phase encodes displacement). Lie group framework: Gao et al. 2021 (recurrent transformation as matrix Lie group, hexagonal from isotropic scaling), Dorrell et al. 2022 (hexagonal grid is optimal under non-negative, bounded, precise coding constraints via group representation theory). non-spatial: Constantinescu et al. 2016 hexagonal fMRI in conceptual spaces, Aronov et al. 2017 in auditory frequency
- mechanisms/path_integration.md: dead reckoning: x_{t+1} = x_t + v_t * dt. mathematical formulation: x(t) = x(0) + integral v*cos(theta) dt, y(t) = y(0) + integral v*sin(theta) dt. biological implementation: head direction cells (Taube et al. 1990, postsubiculum/ADN, ring attractor, vestibular angular velocity integration, ~60-90 degree tuning width, drift ~1-5 deg/min in darkness), speed cells (Kropff et al. 2015, MEC layers III/V/VI, linear rate-speed relationship, 50-80 ms prospective bias), conjunctive grid x HD cells (combine "where in grid" with "which direction moving" for velocity decomposition). error accumulation: ~1-2 cm/m in darkness (Hardcastle et al. 2015). correction by: visual landmarks (fast reset), boundary information (border cells), inter-module consistency (incommensurate spacings enable error detection). CAN path integration: velocity shifts activity bumps. OI path integration: velocity modulates VCO frequency, displacement encoded in phase. Banino et al. 2018: recurrent net trained on path integration spontaneously develops grid-like representations, enables vector-based navigation surpassing human performance
- mechanisms/cognitive_maps.md: Tolman 1948 cognitive maps in rats, O'Keefe & Nadel 1978 hippocampal implementation, Eichenbaum relational memory (hippocampus encodes relationships between items/events/contexts, not just spatial). convergence: hippocampal-entorhinal system builds graph-structured representations (nodes = states, edges = transitions, metric from transition structure). Whittington et al. 2020 Tolman-Eichenbaum Machine (TEM): structural knowledge (MEC analog, learned transition structure basis) + sensory knowledge (hippocampal analog, bindings to observations). TEM attention analogous to transformer self-attention with learned positional encoding. emergent cell types: grid-like units, place-like units, band/border/object-vector cells. non-spatial evidence: conceptual spaces (Constantinescu 2016, hexagonal fMRI for bird morphology), social maps (Tavares 2015, power-affiliation 2D space), task state spaces (Schuck 2016). successor representations (Stachenfeld et al. 2017): place cells encode expected future occupancy, grid cells are eigenvectors of the graph Laplacian. graph-theoretic formalization: spectral properties of transition graph determine grid-like basis functions

comparison:
- comparisons/pga_vs_grid_cells.md: 5-dimension structured comparison of todorov G(3,0,1) PGA and grid cell computations. (1) mathematical structure: grid cells use Abelian 2D translation group representations, PGA encodes full non-Abelian 3D Euclidean group -- shared at "both involve groups" level only (WEAK). (2) periodicity: grid cells fundamentally periodic (hexagonal lattice is defining property), PGA operations NOT periodic -- FUNDAMENTAL MISMATCH. (3) dimensionality: grid cells 2D, PGA 3D+projective -- the relevant algebra for grid cells would be G(2,0,1) or complex numbers, not G(3,0,1). (4) learned vs hardwired: grid patterns emerge from network topology (attractor dynamics) in first minutes of exploration, PGA projections learned from data via gradient descent. (5) computational role: grid cells compute temporal integral (path integration, recurrent), PGA computes instantaneous bilinear function (self-interaction, no recurrence). verdict: WEAK to NONEXISTENT at implementation level, SPECULATIVE at theoretical level. critical missing experiment: GP vs random bilinear operation at matched parameters -- if performance equivalent, geometric structure is irrelevant. dissenting argument: PGA as structured inductive bias for relational computation (analogous to CNN weight sharing for translation equivariance), consistent with MI improvement, but untested

the most actionable proposal is progressive spike activation: training without ternary spikes during warmup, then linearly ramping spikes over ~10% of training. this addresses the known optimization difficulty of the STE gradient by letting the network establish a good representation geometry in continuous space before quantizing. the biological analog is the developmental period before GABA maturation, when circuits are active but not yet subject to inhibitory competition. estimated probability of meaningful BPB improvement: 20-30%. this is a SINGLE CHANGE that can be validated at 6m scale before deploying at 300m.

the weakest claim to stress-test: "progressive spike activation will improve BPB." counterarguments: (1) the continuous-space representations may not survive quantization (the network learns to use regions of activation space that become ambiguous at the ternary boundary), (2) the STE bias during the ramp phase could destabilize training, (3) 10% of training steps for the ramp may be wasted if the representations have to be relearned. the strongest counterargument is (1): if the network learns representations optimized for continuous computation, the forced discretization at the end of the ramp is a form of catastrophic intervention, not graceful maturation. biological development avoids this because the GABA excitatory-to-inhibitory switch is gradual (KCC2 expression increases over weeks), not abrupt.

what about the STE as an analog to developmental backpropagation of action potentials (BAP)? BAPs are real phenomena in pyramidal neurons: somatic action potentials propagate backward into the dendrite, providing a dendritic calcium signal that enables STDP. but BAPs are NOT a gradient signal -- they do not carry error information. they provide a timing signal (postsynaptic spike time) that gates Hebbian plasticity. the STE, by contrast, passes the upstream gradient through the quantization step unchanged. these are fundamentally different computations: BAPs enable local, Hebbian plasticity by providing a coincidence signal; the STE enables global, gradient-based optimization by ignoring the quantization nonlinearity. the analogy is purely superficial (both involve "backward" information flow through a nonlinearity).

## 2026-04-06 dendritic computation domain | mechanisms, bridge, entities, simulation

mechanism articles:
- mechanisms/dendritic_computation.md: London & Hausser 2005 review. dendrites as active computational elements, not passive cables. six computational primitives: supralinear summation (local dendritic spikes amplify coincident inputs beyond linear sum), sublinear summation (shunting creates local divisive normalization at single-branch level), coincidence detection (narrow temporal windows for dendritic spike initiation: ~1-2 ms Na+, ~5-10 ms NMDA, ~10-100 ms Ca2+), directional selectivity (passive cable properties + active conductance distribution creates sequence sensitivity), compartmentalized processing (thin dendrites ~0.5-2 um diameter create ~30-50 semi-independent processing units per neuron), local plasticity (branch-specific STDP based on local voltage/calcium/neuromodulation). cable equation formulation with active conductances. cortical layer of N neurons provides ~30N nonlinear subunits, not N point integrators. computational implications: neuron is not a perceptron, dendritic gating is multiplicative interaction, dendritic computation enables credit assignment (Sacramento et al. 2018), compartmentalized processing enables multiplexing
- mechanisms/dendritic_spikes.md: three types of dendritic spikes, each with different biophysical mechanisms and computational properties. Na+ spikes: fast (~1-2 ms), proximal, low threshold, fast coincidence detection. Ca2+ spikes: slow (~10-100 ms), apical tuft/CaIZ, high threshold, BAC firing mechanism, burst output encoding top-down/bottom-up coincidence. BAC firing: somatic AP backpropagates to CaIZ, IF coincident with apical depolarization from top-down input triggers dendritic Ca2+ spike, which propagates back to soma producing burst (2-5 spikes). ## 2026-04-06 consciousness and global workspace domain | mechanisms, comparison, bridge, entities, simulation

mechanism articles:
- mechanisms/global_workspace_theory.md: Baars 1988, Dehaene & Naccache 2001. the brain has many specialized processors (visual, auditory, motor, memory, emotional) operating unconsciously in parallel. consciousness arises when information from one processor is broadcast to a global workspace accessible by ALL processors. theater metaphor: stage (workspace), spotlight (attention), audience (unconscious processors). neural implementation (Dehaene, Changeux): workspace neurons are large pyramidal cells in L2/3 and L5 with long-range connections, concentrated in prefrontal-parietal cortex. broadcast triggered by ignition -- nonlinear, all-or-none activation of workspace network. capacity limited (~4 items). evidence: masking (P3b absent for unseen stimuli), attentional blink (workspace occupied), binocular rivalry (competition for workspace access). neural signatures: P3b ERP (~300 ms), late gamma burst, prefrontal sustained activation, long-range synchrony. criticisms: does not address hard problem, capacity/bandwidth underspecified, attention-consciousness relationship contested, 2023 adversarial collaboration partially favored IIT
- mechanisms/integrated_information_theory.md: Tononi 2004. consciousness IS integrated information (Phi). five axioms about experience (intrinsicality, information, integration, exclusion, composition) mapped to five physical postulates. Phi = minimum information lost when system is partitioned at its weakest link. mathematical formulation: ii(s) via KL divergence of cause-effect repertoire vs unconstrained distribution, phi(s) = min_partition [ii(s) - ii_partitioned(s)], big Phi = sum across all distinctions and relations. four versions (1.0-4.0). predictions: feedforward networks have Phi=0, cerebellum not conscious, consciousness in posterior cortex (not prefrontal). 2023 adversarial collaboration: IIT 2/3, GWT 0/3 (contested). criticisms: computational intractability (super-exponential, Bell numbers), panpsychism (Aaronson XOR gates), unfalsifiability (identity claim). comparison table with GWT across 8 dimensions
- mechanisms/ignition_dynamics.md: Dehaene et al. 2005, 2014. ignition = sudden, self-sustaining activation of widespread cortical network when local signal exceeds threshold AND attention is available. three phases: local feedforward processing (0-200 ms, identical for seen/unseen), threshold crossing (signal strength + attentional availability), ignition (~200-300 ms, nonlinear bifurcation). cellular substrate: L2/3 and L5 pyramidal neurons, feedforward AMPA, feedback NMDA (voltage-dependent Mg2+ block creates threshold nonlinearity). properties: nonlinear (saddle-node bifurcation), all-or-none (bimodal distribution), self-sustaining (~200-300 ms reverberant activity), exclusive (one representation broadcasts, competitors suppressed). neural signatures: P3b, late gamma, long-range synchrony (beta feedback, gamma feedforward), sustained PFC. subliminal processing: feedforward semantic/emotional/motor processing without ignition (Marcel 1983, Whalen 1998). criticisms: cause vs consequence debate, is it truly all-or-none, Lamme recurrent processing theory
- mechanisms/thalamocortical_loops.md: thalamus as relay AND regulator. relay neurons have two modes: tonic (faithful relay, waking) and burst (change detector, sleep, T-type Ca2+ channels). Sherman & Guillery first-order vs higher-order distinction: first-order relays receive subcortical drivers (LGN<-retina, VP<-spinal cord), higher-order relays receive L5 cortical drivers (pulvinar, POm, MD) creating transthalamic cortex-to-cortex routing. most thalamus by volume is higher-order. TRN: GABAergic inhibitory shell, topographically organized, receives from both thalamocortical and corticothalamic axons, sends inhibition back to relay nuclei, implements selective gating (push-pull: facilitate one relay, inhibit another). Crick 1984: TRN as attentional searchlight. consciousness: bilateral thalamic lesions -> coma, TRN lesions -> akinetic mutism, DBS of central thalamus restores consciousness (Schiff 2007). thalamocortical dysrhythmia (Llinas 1999): stuck burst mode -> aberrant theta -> tinnitus/pain/depression. sleep: tonic-to-burst transition blocks sensory relay, spindle oscillations (12-15 Hz), REM restores tonic mode (ACh) enabling dreams

- comparisons/gwt_vs_transformer.md: GWT (specialized processors + global workspace + ignition) vs transformer (layers + residual stream + attention). 6 dimensions: shared workspace (residual stream IS shared but LACKS capacity limits), broadcast mechanism (passive additive accumulation vs active selective ignition), selectivity (selective reading via attention but non-selective writing), nonlinearity/threshold (no ignition threshold, every layer always writes), information integration (implicit superposition vs explicit workspace combination), recurrence (KDA within-layer vs GWT workspace-level reverberant loops).
